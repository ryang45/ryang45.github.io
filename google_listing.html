Getting A Good Google Listing

Achieving a good Google listing requires knowledge, patience, persistence, and sometimes a little bit of luck.

Users seek out Google because of search relevance. Google attempts to maintain unbiased and relevant search results by constantly updating their search algorithms. Search engine optimizers study the results to gain knowledge of how Google is currently ranking sites.

What does it take to get and maintain a good Google listing?

  • • Patience! It may take up to 120 days to see movement in results.
  • • Design a site with relevant content.
  • • Optimize the title, description, keywords, and text.
  • • Be sure that the site is designed for easy navigation not only by users but also by Google's spider.
  • • Obtain incoming links from related websites.
  • • Obtain a listing in The Open Directory (free) and the Yahoo Directory (paid). Consider other relevant directories.
  • • Check to see what competitive sites are doing.
  • • Monitor results, refine as needed.
  • • If it makes economic sense for your targeted keywords, consider using Adwords, which is pay per click advertising.

Now that we have said what to do, here are some pitfalls to avoid:

  • • Be careful of inter-linking sites, if you have multiple sites with similar content, Google may drop all but one.
  • • If you link exchange with friends, business associates, or community, watch out. Google invented the Page Rank system which relies on incoming links. Now that everyone has caught on to it, Google is trying to change the system to prevent spam. Unfortunately, the continual changes sometimes penalize legitimate sites.
  • • Avoid black hat SEO - hidden text, doorways, re-directs, and cloaking. These techniques have always been considered inappropriate and might get you penalized by the search engines.
